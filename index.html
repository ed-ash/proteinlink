The Problem

Ghana produces enough protein.
It just never reaches the right hands.

Informal middlemen, absent cold chains, and zero traceability mean modern buyers can't get consistent, safe protein at scale — and farmers lose up to 40% of their margins.

💸

Middlemen Absorb 40%

No direct market access. Brokers extract margins farmers can't afford to lose.

🧊

Cold Chain Doesn't Exist

Over 20% spoilage post-slaughter. Temperature breaks lock out Shoprite, Melcom, and hotels.

📦

No Traceability

Modern retailers demand farm-to-fork visibility. Ghana's informal sector has none.

The Solution

One platform.
Every link in the chain.

ProteinLink owns and operates the processing, cold storage, and distribution layers — so nothing gets lost between farm and fork.

01 🌾

Aggregate

WhatsApp-native network connects 50+ smallholder farmers. Daily price signals. Mobile money payments.

02 🏭

Process

HACCP-aligned, species-separated slaughterhouse. Full traceability from farm gate to shelf.

03 🧊

Cold Chain

Temperature-logged cold storage with IoT monitoring. Zero excuses for spoilage.

04 🚛

Distribute

Refrigerated last-mile to Shoprite, Melcom, hotels, and institutions across Accra.

Our Story

Two founders.
One shared mission.

Edmund and Kojo met in the maiden Ashesi MBA cohort and realised their careers had been quietly converging on the same problem.

Kojo had spent years moving pork, catfish, and beef across Accra's busiest channels — building a 55-acre piggery and co-running one of Ghana's leading marketing agencies, behind campaigns including Twitter's first Ghana activation.

Edmund came from a decade in telecom, regulatory consulting, and edtech. In 2024 he started farming, and within four months secured a 3MT/month anchor beef contract with the biggest food chain in Ghana.

"We're not building from scratch — we're bringing together a combined decade of complementary experience. Kojo's deep operational roots in Ghana's livestock trade. Edmund's technology and commercial strategy. The result is ProteinLink."
